Tim and Nicole met one month before Tim was moving 1856 miles to Boulder, CO. It was love at first sight for Tim who made his mother wait hours in the driveway of a friend's graduation party so he could work up the courage to ask for Nicole's phone number.

Over the next four years, Tim took countless cross-country flights to never miss a Valentine's Day in Connecticut & always successfully surprise Nicole for her sorority formals. Tim even went so far as to swap his phone with his mother's so she could send text messages pretending to be Tim to keep Nicole from suspecting anything while he was actually flying across the country!

They survived living thousands of miles apart for four years, a hundred miles apart for two more while Nicole finished grad school and then 4.2 miles apart for a few more years. They finally decided to move in together & the rest is history!

The Proposal

"I really love you"
— Timmy. A true master of words.

Tim convinced Nicole to take December 23, 2016, off to spend the evening in New York City. They planned to have dinner in the city and then see the Christmas Spectacular, a show Nicole had been begging Tim to take her to for many years. On the morning of the 23rd, Tim mentioning that Nicole should probably go to the spa to relax and get her nails done as she would most likely not have time before Christmas, not mentioning that she would be showing off her new ring the next day.

They hopped on the train and Tim played it cool as they chatted nonchalantly about things like Nicole wanting to make sure she saw Home Alone this Christmas season as she did not get the opportunity to last year. When they arrived in the city, Tim mentioned they had some time before their dinner reservation, at an undisclosed location, so they decided to head over to the Rockefeller tree. After a romantic viewing of the tree where Tim spent a longer amount than usual holding Nicole tight while they looked up at the lights, they ventured over to the restaurant for a drink before dinner.

As Nicole followed Tim to the undisclosed location, they started to approach darkness and Nicole thought to herself 'I wonder if Tim is planning to murder me', very romantic. It turned out to be Central Park where they were headed so she was thinking the restaurant must be on the other side. The stroll through the park was magical with people ice skating on various ponds and Christmas music playing throughout. At one point, Tim stopped and called Nicole over to a bridge and pointed to the lit up building across the street and asked if Nicole recognized the Plaza Hotel from Home Alone, which she mentioned earlier in the day.

As they were on the edge of the bridge overlooking the Plaza Hotel, Tim put his arms around Nicole and said he loved her, to which Nicole responded 'I love you too.' At that point, Tim spun her around to face him and said 'No Nicole, I love you and have loved you since the day I met you' then he got down on one knee, pulled out a little black box, and asked her to marry him. Through the obvious tears, Nicole managed to say Yes! Tim then surprised Nicole with the dinner location, which was their favorite restaurant, the R Lounge, overlooking Times Square and informed her that five years ago to the day he took her to the R Lounge for the first time.

They enjoyed the first of many bottles of champagne that Christmas season with dinner and headed over to the Christmas Spectacular as the future Mr. & Mrs. Garaffa. One scene in the show shows a couple ice skating in front of a bridge which they realized was their bridge! It was the most spectacular evening to start off the rest of their life together. Not only did Nicole finally get to see the Christmas Spectacular, but she got to see it with her newly established future husband!
